Recommendation

Intro

In the podcast, Crazy/Genius by The Atlantic’s Derek Thompson, a strikingly good episode in the series is the episode “Can Artificial Intelligence Be Smarter Than a Human?” in Season 2, Episode 5 of the series. This episode was very interesting and different from the other episodes in the series- in a good way. Thompson takes a difficult and very intricate topic, artificial intelligence, and explains it in an accessible way that hooks the reader and engages them. Thompson takes this complex topic and introduces it in a way that can help readers appreciate the technology and understand the full grasp of it. In addition to this, the producers bring in various professionals in the field that help explain their own take on the topic and bring in their own perspectives. And to wrap it all up, the producers use different artistic techniques in order to elevate important information and make it even more engaging.

Author's Craft

One of the best things about this podcast is the way the topic is explained. The producers in this podcast episode seemed to understand the importance of music; and the importance of silence. Throughout the episode, the producers use light background music during conversations making the reader feel as though they are there with Thompson and the interviewee. Just as importantly, the producers use silence to elevate the episode. Many podcasts use a lot of music and a lot of effects leading to sensory overload and making it hard for the listeners to discern between important information and fluff. In this podcast episode however, the producers know the importance of silence, and use it to their advantage. The producers use silence and very light music after asking hard questions to give the listeners time to reflect and process the information and questions being asked. One such moment that the producers do this is about halfway through the podcast when Thompson says “, It can discover answers that humans can't even imagine. It can do things we can't. Does that mean the machine is smarter than its makers?” which is then followed by silence. This is a crucially important part of the podcast because it helps the readers process the information and deeply think about the complex topic at hand. These silent breaks are important and elevate this technology podcast compared to others because they help the reader think and come up with their own conclusions based on the given information. It’s very organic, and the episode promotes your own thinking, which is why it is such a great episode.

Content Explanation

Another great thing about this episode is the content within it. This podcast takes such a complex topic and explains it in a comprehensive way. Thompson takes this vast and intricate topic and explains the different parts of it by explaining technical vocabulary. One such example in the podcast where a complex definition is broken down into simpler terms is when they explain Machine Learning, saying that “Machine learning is like a brilliant baby. It absorbs everything, it does a few things well- but it understands just nothing.” This is just one example where Thompson takes a difficult topic and makes it easier for non-experts to understand. Rather than steer away from explaining the complex terms and actually explaining the topic in depth, Thompson takes it and makes it understandable. It’s very approachable and accessible for anyone, which is why it’s a great episode. This podcast is also great with it’s topic because it provides the opinions and voices of real experts in the field. Many podcasts often don’t provide the voices of enough people, which can harm the listeners' understanding of the topic. It is important to include the opinions of other people, especially in such a broad and new field. And this podcast does that. It uses the voices from companies that use artificial intelligence, programmers that use artificial intelligence, regular people that use artificial intelligence so it can provide it’s listeners with a broad understanding of the topic. This is what makes this podcast so great; it isn’t just one person’s opinion or one person’s own line of thinking, it’s a collective group of people from different places and with different goals telling their own take on artificial intelligence and their own experiences with it. You need the experiences of others in this field. You need the different goals people have with artificial intelligence. This is why this is such a great episode.

Conclusion

To wrap it up, a great episode in the podcast, Crazy/Genius by The Atlantic’s Derek Thompson is the episode “Can Artificial Intelligence Be Smarter Than a Human?” in Season 2, Episode 5 of the series. The producers use good craft moves, they explain the topic in an accessible and easy-to-understand way, and they use the voices and experiences of others in order to teach the listener about artificial intelligence.
